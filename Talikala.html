Talikala Foundation

    Talikala, an institution founded by Cindy O’Donnell, Elizabeth O’Brien, and Felicidad Prieto on August 7, 1987, at Davao City. Its primary concern way back was the health and welfare of prostituted women and children affected by HIV/AIDS. As their institution grew, the expanse of their goal reached to providing families with well-tended education, personality enhancement, legal assistance, and a lot more. In addition, their development also stood as an inspiration to those organizations that are merely starting to grow and hope to attend to the issues of the community. Their 30 year worth of success led them to receive the Drs. Jess and Trining de la Paz Award as a form of appreciation and acknowledgment.

STORY OF THE INSTITUTION

       Talikala, Inc. used to be based on August 7, 1987 with the aid of three girls of numerous backgrounds: American lay missionary Cindy O’Donnell, Filipina social employee Elizabeth O’Brien, and former night time membership dancer Felicidad Prieto. Originally, the team was once shaped to assist defend the prostituted ladies and teens in Davao City from the disaster wreaked by means of HIV/AIDS, however it has for the reason that grown in a position to reply to the task of completely emancipating these exploited girls and kids from the bondage of human trafficking and prostitution. Current govt director Ms. Jeanette Laurel-Ampog, for example, is a important voice of the opposition to the decriminalization of prostitution, putting forward that solely the prostitute herself has to be decriminalized, and now not the profession.

ACTIVITES IN THE INSTITUTION

The different activities in the institution includes; improving access to social services and livelihood opportunities for disadvantaged communities and victims of trafficking, the training of community-based groups, government agencies and service providers to guard against trafficking, and implement current policies and introduce new measures to guard against trafficking.
